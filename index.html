Food Security Across New York City

Oscar Flores
December 23, 2024



NEW YORK, NY - Food insecurity affects families across all five boroughs, especially in neighborhoods like the Bronx, Brooklyn, and Queens, where access to fresh, affordable food is limited. Many residents struggle to find healthy options due to high prices and a lack of nearby grocery stores. However, farmers markets are offering a solution. These markets provide fresh produce at lower prices, often accepting SNAP benefits to help low-income families. They also work with food banks and community groups to distribute free groceries to those in need.

BronxWorks, a nonprofit serving the Bronx, plays have their own markets. "Nutrition is really important to emphasize, especially for people who are struggling or don't have access to healthy food," said Bianca Cornelious, a BronxWorks Coordinator. "Through our SNAP-ED program, we're focusing on individuals enrolled in SNAP, teaching them how to eat healthy on a budget." These markets and programs are helping fight food insecurity and improve nutrition across the city
